01 · Introduction

About This Policy

This Privacy Policy describes how Shubham Jada ("I," "me," or "Developer") collects, uses, and shares information when you use any of my applications on the Apple App Store.

One policy covers all my Apps — current and future. By using any of my Apps, you agree to this policy.

I will never sell your data, use it for undisclosed purposes, or share it beyond what is described here. What you read is exactly what happens.
02 · Collection

What We Collect

You provide

  • Name, email, username, password — only if an App requires an account
  • Content you create or submit within the App
  • Support messages and feedback

Collected automatically

  • Device model, OS version, and identifiers
  • Crash logs and anonymous diagnostic data
  • IP address and approximate region (country/state — never precise GPS)
  • Advertising Identifier (IDFA) — only if you grant ATT permission

What we never collect

  • Precise location, contacts, camera, or microphone — without explicit permission
  • Biometric data of any kind
  • Payment card or bank details — Apple handles all billing
  • Data from children under 13, knowingly

05 · Advertising

Google AdMob & Advertising

Third-party technology — When ads are shown, Google controls ad targeting data collection under their own Privacy Policy at policies.google.com/privacy

Apps display Banner, Interstitial, Rewarded, Native, and App Open ads. All ads are clearly labeled. Ad content rating is set per App's App Store age rating (G / PG / T / MA).

What AdMob may collect

  • Advertising Identifier (IDFA) — only with your ATT permission
  • IP address and approximate region
  • Device info and ad interaction data

Your controls

  • EU/EEA users: Google UMP consent dialog shown before any ads load
  • All users: Apple ATT prompt shown before any IDFA tracking — declining still gives full App access with non-personalized ads (npa=1)
  • Premium subscribers: ads completely disabled, no AdMob data collected
  • Change ATT: Settings → Privacy & Security → Tracking → [App Name]
  • Opt out of Google ads: adssettings.google.com
06 · Subscriptions

In-App Purchases & Subscriptions

I never see or store your payment card information. All billing is handled entirely by Apple.
  • Manage or cancel: Settings → [Your Name] → Subscriptions
  • Free trials auto-convert to paid unless cancelled before the trial ends
  • Subscriptions auto-renew unless cancelled at least 24 hours before renewal
  • Refunds: reportaproblem.apple.com
  • I receive only a transaction ID and subscription status — no financial data
  • Active subscribers have advertising completely disabled
07 · Features

Notifications & Widgets

Push notifications

  • Explicit iOS permission always requested before any notification is sent
  • Disable anytime: Settings → Notifications → [App Name]

Widgets

08 · Deletion

Account & Data Deletion

Apple App Store Requirement

Per Apple's Guideline 5.1.1(v), Apps with accounts must provide in-app deletion. Apps without this are rejected since 2023.

For Apps with accounts: navigate to App Settings → Account → Delete Account. Account and personal data are deleted within 30 days. For Apps without accounts, deleting the App removes all data from your device immediately.

You may also request deletion by emailing moonarkh.dev@gmail.com.

11 · Children & GDPR

Children's Privacy & Global Compliance

My Apps are not directed to children under 13 (or 16 in the EU) unless explicitly stated in the App Store listing.

If you believe I have collected data from a child under 13, contact moonarkh.dev@gmail.com immediately. I will delete it within 48 hours.

Child-directed Apps configure AdMob with isChildDirectedApp = true — COPPA-compliant, no behavioral targeting, no IDFA, G-rated ads only. EU users under digital consent age have UMP disabled via tagForUnderAgeOfConsent = true.

GDPR — European users

Legal bases: Contractual Necessity, Legitimate Interests, Consent (for personalized ads and notifications), and Legal Obligation. Withdraw consent anytime via in-App "Manage Consent." Lodge complaints with your local Data Protection Authority (DPA).
